Research

I develop mechanistic, spatially explicit modeling frameworks to understand how species interactions shape invasion dynamics and community structure in heterogeneous landscapes. My work bridges ecological theory with management-relevant science through collaborations with USGS and USDA programs in the Greater Everglades ecosystem.

Theme 1 · Dispersal, coexistence, and spatial theory

I compare modeling perspectives (e.g., deterministic state-variable models, reaction–diffusion formulations, and stochastic individual-based models) to understand how different assumptions can produce different predictions for coexistence and dispersal-driven outcomes.

  • Model-structure effects on coexistence and competitive outcomes
  • Mechanisms generating spatial heterogeneity and persistence
  • Connecting conceptual theory to testable predictions

Theme 2 · Invasion dynamics, biocontrol, and restoration

I apply theory-grounded models to invasive species management questions, including scenario evaluation and biocontrol effectiveness. A major focus is the Greater Everglades ecosystem.

  • Spatial invasion dynamics and landscape heterogeneity
  • Biocontrol strategies and interacting stressors (including host–pathogen dynamics)
  • Decision-relevant metrics for restoration planning

Current funded collaborations

USGS · Greater Everglades Priority Ecosystem Science Program

Modeling vegetation and invasion dynamics to support restoration planning and risk assessment.

  • Spatially explicit individual-based modeling of invasion impacts
  • Climate-driven metapopulation modeling of range expansion

USDA · Invasive species / biocontrol program

Developing agent-based modeling tools to evaluate biocontrol effectiveness and explore synergistic outcomes under interacting stressors.

  • Scenario experiments and uncertainty analysis
  • Management-relevant thresholds and intervention windows

Methods & tools

  • Modeling: individual-based models, metapopulation models, stochastic processes, spatial simulation
  • Programming: MATLAB, R, Python
  • Workflow: reproducible research, Git/GitHub, high-performance computing (HiPerGator)
